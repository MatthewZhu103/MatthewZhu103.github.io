Experience

Machine Learning-Based Retinal Image Enhancement with OTE-GAN

During the summer, I worked with Dr. Wang in researching methods to better enhance retinal images from current models with GAN (Generative Adversarial Networks) based image-to-image translation to better preserve artifacts and improve image quality. I helped to refactor some of the code to work with EyeQ (an image quality classifier) to read data from a csv file

DORA CubeSat

While working as an electrical volunteer for the Interplanetary Lab Initiative, I assessed an electronic power system's performance by conducting battery capacity tests to identify discharge rate issues and fix faulty over and undervoltage protections. Additionally, I collaborated with a mentor to integrate CubeSat systems to provide critical flight data and satellite functionality to ground station equipment. My role involved meticulous documentation, progress updates, and writing testing procedures, ensuring clear communication and effective data transfer.

Sun Devil Robotics Club - Mars Rover

To help the SDRC compete in the University Rover Challenge, I helped make the firmware for the motors to control the rover based on the ODrive motor’s data sheet using a power supply and the provided software on Ubuntu as well as soldering connections between the motors and the motor drivers

UPCOMING

Clap-on Lights w/Clap Recognition

Implementing an Arduino-powered LED system that responds only to claps can be challenging due to interference from other loud noises like fireworks (college students doing college student things), the house settling, and random bangs from me or my neighbors. To solve this, I plan to use an audio classifier to accurately recognize claps and ignore other sounds, ensuring the lights turn on only when I clap

DHT11 & LCD Raw Drivers

As a learning experience, I developed a DHT11 and LCD driver on an ARM Cortex STM32 without using any existing libraries. I had to utilize a logic analyzer, the DHT datasheet, and the LCDs datasheet to decode the communication protocols and for debugging of firmware issues to display the temperature on the LCD

Automatic Dog Feeder

I forget to feed my dog sometimes from the amount of homework and studying I have. I designed a dog feeder based on an Arduino Uno by writing drivers and making a PCB using KiCAD for the components. The dog feeder features an LCD with a menu and also is remote controllable.
